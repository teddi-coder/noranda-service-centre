Why Car AC Stops Working

Refrigerant gas loss is the most common reason your AC stops cooling properly.

Even healthy AC systems lose small amounts of refrigerant gas over time. As levels drop, the compressor has to work harder, putting wear on the whole system. In WA summers, a poorly charged AC system simply cannot keep up — and the cabin gets uncomfortably hot.

A regas restores refrigerant levels, relieves pressure on the compressor, and gets your system working properly again. Most vehicles need a regas every one to two years.
