Why Car Air Conditioning Stops Working

Over time, every car's air conditioning system loses refrigerant gas through normal wear on seals and connections. Once the gas level drops, the system can no longer cool air effectively — leaving you sweltering in Perth's summer heat. A regas restores the refrigerant to the correct level so your AC blows cold again.

If left too long, low refrigerant forces the compressor to work harder, which can lead to more expensive repairs down the track. A routine regas is one of the most cost-effective ways to maintain your AC system.
