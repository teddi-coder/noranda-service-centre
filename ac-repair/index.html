Why Car AC Stops Working Over Time

Most car AC problems come down to one of two things — refrigerant loss or compressor strain.

Over time, refrigerant gas slowly leaks from the system — even in healthy vehicles. When levels drop, the system works harder to cool the cabin, putting strain on the compressor and other components. Left too long, a simple regas becomes a costly repair.

WA summers make this worse. High ambient temperatures push AC systems to their limit, accelerating wear on seals and components. A regas every year or two keeps your system healthy and your cabin cool.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is my car AC not cold?

The most common cause is low refrigerant gas. Over time, small amounts leak from the system even without a visible fault. A regas restores the gas level and typically has you cold again within an hour.
